# 🎓 Gorrito Learning

Welcome to **Gorrito Learning**, the official open-source sandbox and learning laboratory for **@gorritocodes**. 

I’m building this organization to document my journey from a capable JavaScript/TypeScript developer to a tech entrepreneur. Right now, I'm 33 years old. I know where I want to go, and I know exactly what I need to master to get there. 

I am building and breaking things completely in public because, in a couple of years, no one is going to believe I started here. 🚀

---

## 🎯 The Philosophy: One Stack, Infinite Shots
My entrepreneurial strategy is simple: **Learn one tool for each job, master it deeply, and use that exact same stack to build product after product.** Maybe my first SaaS will fail, or the second won't be "the one"—but by maintaining a single, hyper-efficient stack, I can keep taking shots until I win.

> 📝 **Note:** You won't find my actual SaaS products here. This organization is strictly my gym. It is for tutorials, tests, trials, isolated components, deep-dive practices, and countless hours of reading documentation and building. Check the commit history if you want proof of the grind!

---

## 🗻 The Mission
My ultimate goal is to build companies using a professional, enterprise-grade stack. As a realistic entrepreneur, I also look at this as the ultimate hedge: if an idea doesn't work out, I will have built a world-class portfolio of skills and projects to confidently land a top-tier industry job. This organization holds the receipts of that evolution.

## 🧱 The Core Stack 
These are the foundational pillars I am mastering to build my future tech ecosystem. If it's on this list, it is a non-negotiable part of my toolkit. Here is where I currently stand with each:

*   **TypeScript:** Moving past basic types to master complex, reusable generic features.
*   **React & React Native:** I understand the core concepts but am focusing on mastering `useRef`, advanced context management, and adapting to native components (`<View>` vs `<div>`).
*   **Next.js & Expo:** I have a basic understanding of both frameworks and their routers, but I am working to transition from a novice to an advanced user.
*   **Node.js, Express & NestJS:** Capable of building basic REST APIs, but actively leveling up on authentication, advanced middleware, and enterprise-grade NestJS patterns.
*   **MongoDB & PostgreSQL:** I am comfortable with MongoDB, but currently a beginner when it comes to SQL and relational databases.

---

## 🚀 The Support Toolkit (Learning on the Go)
To support my core stack and ensure professional deployments, I am also integrating and mastering these tools as I build:

*   **Styles & UI:** This is my biggest current challenge. I am actively working to transition away from purely relying on AI-generated styling to building custom, solid layouts myself.
*   **DevOps & CI/CD:** Learning **Docker** for containerization, and mastering Git, **GitHub Actions**, and **Husky** for professional deployment pipelines.
*   **Tooling & Ecosystem:** Implementing **Prisma ORM** (for Postgres), **Zod** (for schema validation), **Clerk** (for Auth), and upgrading my **Jest** foundation into end-to-end testing with **Playwright**.
*   **Linters & Formatters:** Utilizing **Prettier** and **ESLint** to enforce clean code, while diving deeper into advanced configurations.
*   **IDE & Development Environment (DX):** I believe every developer on my future team should be able to plug-and-play any app I develop. The goal is to minimize global installs, keep dependencies minimal, and ensure a flawless local developer experience.
